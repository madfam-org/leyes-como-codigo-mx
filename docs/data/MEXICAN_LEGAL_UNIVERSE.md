## Legal Hierarchy (Art. 133 Constitucional)

Mexico's legal framework follows a strict hierarchy established by Article 133 of the Constitution:

```
1. Constitución Política de los Estados Unidos Mexicanos
2. Tratados Internacionales (ratified, per SCJN thesis)
3. Leyes Federales + Leyes Generales
4. Constituciones Estatales
5. Leyes Estatales
6. Reglamentos (federal, state, municipal)
7. Bandos Municipales, Normas Oficiales Mexicanas, Decretos, Acuerdos, Circulares
```

The principle of **jerarquía normativa** means higher-tier instruments prevail over lower-tier ones in case of conflict.

**Known count**: 23,660 across 32 states
**Our count**: 18,416 scraped and parsed (77.8%)

### Power Breakdown

| Power ID | Name | Description | Estimated Count |
|----------|------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Poder Ejecutivo | State executive regulations, decrees, agreements | ~12,000 |
| 3 | Poder Judicial | State judicial regulations, internal rules | ~5,000 |
| 4 | Organismos Autónomos | Autonomous body regulations (OPLE, CEDH, etc.) | ~6,660 |

### Important Clarification

These are **state-level** instruments from non-legislative branches — NOT municipal laws. The OJN probe (2026-02-03) confirmed that powers 1/3/4 return state executive orders, judicial administrative rules, and autonomous body regulations.

### Scraping & Parsing Status (Updated 2026-02-07)

- **Scraped**: 18,439 files across 29 states (3 states empty: CDMX, Durango, Zacatecas)
- **Parsed to AKN XML**: 18,416 (99.9% success rate, 23 failures)
- **Articles extracted**: 398,806
- **Infrastructure**: `OJNScraper.scrape_state_comprehensive()` with `power_ids=[1, 2, 3, 4]`
- **Parser**: `scripts/ingestion/parse_state_laws.py --non-legislative --all`
- **Remaining gap**: ~5,244 laws (mostly from states with OJN data quality issues)

### Confidence Levels

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **High** | Verified from official API/catalog with exact count |
| **Medium** | Official source exists but count is approximate or requires interpretation |
| **Low** | No official census; estimate based on available data points |

### Known Limitations

- Municipal counts are rough estimates — no central authority tracks these
- CONAMER CNARTyS count includes instruments that may overlap with other tiers
- Judicial corpus count includes historical epochs that may have limited digital availability
- Treaty count varies by definition (some sources count amendments as separate instruments)
